PROMPT — Step 2 Institutional Rating Sliders
Replace the current Step 2 of the RMCW survey with a new Campus Rating step. The page heading is 'Rate Your Campus' with subtext 'How well did your campus support you in each area? All 8 are required.' A progress indicator shows how many sliders have been touched out of 8.
The step contains 8 cards stacked vertically. Each card follows this exact structure:
- Dimension name as the card header (bold, large) - Short label in smaller text below the name - A slider from 0 to 10. Default position is 0 with no value selected. The slider must be deliberately moved before it registers. Display the numeric score next to the slider. Left label: 'No Support'. Right label: 'Exceptional'. - Below the slider, a collapsed accordion labeled 'What does this include? ▾'. Only one accordion can be open at a time — opening one closes any other currently open. The accordion contains the definition text for that dimension.
The Continue button is inactive until all 8 sliders have been moved from 0. If the student tries to continue without completing all 8, scroll to the first incomplete slider and highlight it with a red border.
Store each slider value in Supabase in its respective column: rating_physical, rating_emotional, rating_intellectual, rating_social, rating_spiritual, rating_environmental, rating_occupational, rating_financial.
The 8 cards in order are:
Card 1 — Physical Label: Nourishment, Rest & Recovery Accordion: Think about access to food (dining halls, food pantries), quality of sleep environments in housing, campus recreation and fitness facilities, and access to student health services when you were sick or recovering.
Card 2 — Emotional Label: Mental Health & Crisis Support Accordion: Think about counseling availability, wait times for appointments, crisis response, how openly mental health is talked about on campus, and whether you felt safe asking for help.
Card 3 — Intellectual Label: Academic Support & Risk Navigation Accordion: Think about tutoring, academic advising quality, early warning systems when you were struggling, access to office hours, academic probation support, and how the campus communicated academic risk to you.
Card 4 — Social Label: Belonging, Inclusion & Community Accordion: Think about whether you felt welcomed, whether you found your people, the quality of residential life, availability of student organizations, and how inclusive the campus culture felt across different identities and backgrounds.
Card 5 — Spiritual Label: Orientation, Purpose & Campus Culture Accordion: Think about the quality of onboarding and orientation, whether student government felt representative, whether campus values aligned with yours, and whether the institution gave you a sense of direction beyond academics.
Card 6 — Environmental Label: Safety, Housing & Campus Navigability Accordion: Think about housing quality and availability, campus security and lighting, ease of getting around, access to quiet and open spaces, and whether the physical environment supported your ability to study and rest.
Card 7 — Occupational Label: Career Direction & Opportunity Access Accordion: Think about career services quality, internship and job placement support, ease of choosing or changing your major, access to research or work opportunities on campus, and whether faculty and advisors helped you connect your studies to your future.
Card 8 — Financial Label: Affordability, Aid & Value Accordion: Think about the clarity and accessibility of financial aid, scholarship availability, the overall burden of cost relative to what you received, and whether the campus helped you understand and manage your financial situation as a student.
Use the same visual style as the existing CampusMind slider questions. Match spacing, font, and card style consistently across all 8 cards.
